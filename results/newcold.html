Oh no, you've got a cold!

The common cold is treatable and preventable. It is a viral infection that affects your nose, throat, and upper respiratory tract. Some of the most common symptoms might be a runny nose, fever, sore throat, cough, sneezing, and congestion. There are some clear indicators of when you should seek medical attention. If you have a temperature of 101.3 or more, Please seek medical attention!

Cold Treatment

Being proactive about your health is important to prevent the common cold from turning into the flu. There are several symptoms that one may expereince when they have the cold. Below are some symotoms and treatment recommendations.

  • Runny Nose
  • Sore Throat
  • Sneezing
  • Congestion
  • Low Grade Fever
  • Wheezing
Preventative Care

Colds are preventable and treatable. Staying active, eating healthy, washing your hands, and more can help you to avoid getting the common cold. Explore the list below for some ways to stay healthy.

  • Vitamins
  • Drink Water
  • Medicinal Teas
  • Exercise
  • Meditation
  • Wash Your Hands
